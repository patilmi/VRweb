    Math

    Rotation Math

    R = A tiny rotation, I = Identity matrix, m = Infinitesmal matrix, v = any vector

    1. R = I + m
    2. ((I + m) v)2 = v2 (Because it's a rigid body transformation)
    3. vT(I + mT)(I + m) v = vTv (Rewrite in matrix form)
    4. vT(I + mT + m) v = vTv (Drop m2 terms)
    5. vT(mT + m) v = 0
    6. mT + m = 0
    7. therefore m must be antisymmetric

    Projection Math

    p = 4D point vector, ŵ = unit vector (0, 0, 0, 1), the 4D eye is also conveniently placed at ŵ, π(p) = Projection of 4D point, s = ball radius scaling due to perspective projection, s' = elongation in π(p) direction

    1. π(p) = (p - (p dot ŵ)) / (1 - (p dot ŵ))
    2. s = 1 / (1 - (p dot w))
    3. s' = √ (1 + π2(p))
