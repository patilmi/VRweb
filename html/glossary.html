FAQs

  1. Q: Is the Fourth Dimension real?

    A: Four dimensional space is just as real as three dimensional space. What we think of as the 'real world' is our brain processing the sensory input it receives. If our brain received the exact same input it receives from the 'real world' but from another source, would it be any less real? Virtual reality is reality that doesn't match up to standards that we are used to in our base reality. If a brain responds to input that corresponds to four dimensional space and is able to generate meaning out of it, then it is real.

  2. Q: Isn't the fourth dimension time?

    A: Anything that can be explored/observed independently of other established dimensions can be counted as an additional dimension. For example on a flat LCD screen, color can be thought of as a third dimension since the whole range of colors is available to each pixel on the screen. In the case of time, the same space existed and will continue to exist regardless of which time it is observed in, and conversely if given a time, the full range of spatial dimension is available to observe, so it too can be thought of as an additional dimension to space.

    3 Dimensions are conceptual, they map conveniently to the experience of what we call the "physical world", which is why they were conceived in the first place.

    Here when we talk about 4D or 4 spatial dimensions, we are talking about four spatial dimensions plus time.

  3. Q: Isn't stereoscopic vision responsible for our depth perception / 3D vision?

    Stereoscopic vision is one of the mechanisms that enables us to see depth. It is particulary useful in scenarious where both observer and object are still, and it enables greater depth perception accuracy within 5 to 20 meters of range. We are focusing on motion parallax as the primary mechanism for space depth perception. (example experiment animation)

    This is further complicated by the 'hacking' of stereo vision to display virtual 3D screen as a fundamental of VR technology.

  4. Q: Even we accept that the brain can construct 3D vision from 2D eye input why should it be able to construct 4D vision from the same 2D eye input?

    A:There is actually nothing inherently two dimensional about the input the eyes supply the brain. The input is actually only one dimensional because it comes from photoreceptors on the retina. These act like point light sensors and the brain has to make sense of them to convert them into vision.

    It is a leap to expect the brain to understand 4D space, but given that it is already taking leaps like producing 3D vision from a collection of point light input, and that our 4D vision learning algorithm is built on the foundation of an already mature 3D vision algorithm, it is reasonable to expect our approach to work.
